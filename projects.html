2024 Projects:

Rocky Brook Reservoir, one of three bodies of water in Tri-pond Park, is located along the main thoroughfare to the village of Peace Dale, in South Kingstown, RI. The Town of South Kingstown Parks and Recreation Department asked the team to focus on the Rocky Brook Pond and its surrounding area with particular attention to addressing the pedestrian access between the existing parking area and a grassy area near the pond, developing the grassy area to be a better community recreational space and improving the existing parking area.

The Jaycees Riverpoint Corridor/Arboretum and Sen. Donald E. Roch Riverwalk is part of the larger Riverpoint Park located in West Warwick, RI. The Town of West Warwick Public Works Department asked the team to focus on the entry to the Riverwalk and portions of the Riverwalk itself with particular attention to addressing the existing entry structure into the Riverwalk, the erosion along the Riverwalk path from the entry point to the fishing area and the fishing area.

In the URI Regenerative Design Lab, faculty and students from the URI Department of Landscape Architecture work in collaboration with the RIIDEM Urban & Community Forestry Program to support RI communities, non-profits, or schools, to develop conceptual green space plans. The long-term strategies of these plans are to conserve, enhance, connect, and protect community natural resources (forests, open space, watersheds, regional trail networks, and riparian corridors). The goal of this collaboration is to support the regeneration of Rhode Island's ecological and human communities to support a more healthy and resilient future in the region.
